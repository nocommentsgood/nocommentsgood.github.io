Legendary Career

  • Played from 1954 to 1976, a 23 year career. Hank Aaron played for the Braves for all but his final two years in the MLB
  • Total of 3,298 games played in the MLB. Third most games played in the MLB.
  • 12,364 at bats. Second most in MLB history.
  • 3771 hits. Third most in MLB history.
  • 2,174 runs. Fourth most in MLB history.
  • 755 home runs. Second most in MLB history.

    • Making a Legend

    Henry Louis Aaron Jr. or Hank Aaron was born February 5th 1934 in Mobile, Alabama. Hank Aaron began playing professional baseball in the Negro League in 1952. Aaron joined the Milwalke Braves in 1954 joining Major League Baseball. This came 8 years after Jackie Robinson integrated the MLB. Aaron quickly became one of the top players in the MLB winning the National League batting title (an achievement of highest batting average) in 1956. In 1957, Hank Aaron was named MVP along with winning the World Series beating Mickey Mantle and the New York Yankees. Hank Aaron set a home run record passing the great Babe Ruth with 715 home runs in 1974. Hank would go on to hit a total of 755 home runs a record that would last until 2007 when Barry Bonds broke the record. Hank Aaron accomplished this and so much more during a time of civil unrest in America. Aaron served as a civil rights activist giving voice to millions of African Americans accross the nation and changing the game of baseball forever.

    Life After Baseball

    Hank Aaron was inducted into the Baseball Hall of Fame in 1982. Aaron gathered 97.8% of the votes on ballots falling shy to Ty Cobb who had 98.2% of votes on the ballot for the very first Hall of Fame election. Hank Aaron was later named VP and director of player development for the Braves making him one of the first minorities in upper level management. Hank went on to climb the upper level management ladder. Hank Aaron founded the Hank Aaron Rookie League program to help those who came after him. Sadly, Hank Aaron died January 22nd at the age of 86.
